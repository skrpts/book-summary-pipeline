## Purpose

Provides grounding for the pipeline's summarisation approach. The techniques below inform how the skills and prompts handle book content.

## Summarisation Principles

### The SQ3R Method

A structured reading approach that maps well to AI-assisted summarisation:

1. **Survey** — Skim headings, subheadings, and structure before reading in depth. The content-extraction skill mirrors this step.
2. **Question** — Formulate questions that each section should answer. The extract-key-ideas prompt uses this framing.
3. **Read** — Process the text with those questions in mind.
4. **Recite** — Summarise each section in your own words. The chapter summarisation stage does this.
5. **Review** — Synthesise across sections and check understanding. The final synthesis stage handles this.

### Bloom's Taxonomy and Summary Depth

Different summary depths map to different cognitive levels:

- **Brief** (Remember/Understand): What are the main facts and ideas?
- **Standard** (Analyse/Apply): How do the ideas connect, and what patterns emerge?
- **Detailed** (Evaluate/Create): What is the quality of the arguments, and what are the implications?

### Common Summarisation Pitfalls

- **Over-generalisation:** Stating "the author discusses X" without capturing what they actually say about it. The prompts explicitly ask for argument content, not just topic labels.
- **Missing the thesis:** Summarising each chapter independently without tracking the book's overarching argument. The synthesis skill addresses this with cross-chapter analysis.
- **Uncritical acceptance:** Summarising what the author claims without noting weak evidence or unstated assumptions. The critical evaluation section counters this.
- **Quote overload:** Substituting direct quotes for genuine understanding. The prompts limit quotes to 2–3 per chapter and require context.

## Academic Referencing

When using summaries in essays or assignments, students should always cite the original book, not the summary. The pipeline includes page/section references where possible to make this easier.
